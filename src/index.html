:: About me

Born in the early 80s, I call myself a digital native. I started programming as I was 16 years old. My first language at that time was Perl and later - as php evolved - I switched to php. I learned nearly everything in an autodidact way - reviewing code of publicly available projects, books or simply by trial and error.

 

As of 2001, I started my apprenticeship as an IT Specialist for application development, which I finished successfully in 2004.

 

In 2005, I started a hobby project related to World of Wacraft, named BLASC. It quickly became the leading source of information in the german speaking territory, for everything game-relevant, e.g. Quests, Items, Spells etc. During early development, 2 friends of mine joined the project and in 2006, Computec Media bought it and employed us as Senior Developers. Out of BLASC, buffed.de was created, but now with an additional editoral staff. Buffed launched in August 2006, during the Games Convention in Leipzig, Germany. With millions of monthly users, buffed.de was one of the largest websites in Germany at this time. More Info: https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buffed

 

During this time, I moved from Ravensburg, to Fuerth, near Nuremberg.

 

In 2009, I co-founded Playata GmbH, a new company specialized in gaming services, like BLASC was. Unfortunately, we realized really fast, that this wasn't a proper business model, so we decided to start things over. Our new goal was to build browser based games with our own engine as a blueprint for further titles to come.

 

After 6 months of intensive work, we've finished the first version of our engine and released Hero Zero. It is still active today and played by millions of players around the world.

 

In 2010, I moved back to Ravensburg and joined Columbus Interactive mid 2012, as a Senior Developer. As the agency continuously grew, I quickly moved into the position of the Technical Director. Since then, I'm responsible for the whole development team and the future of the agency from a technical point of view.

 

I'm generally interested in everything tech related. Lately I rediscovered C# and it's .net ecosystem and have a lot of fun digging my head into it, to get a new perspective besides from php. I love its simplicity but on the same site its feature richness which allows you to build a lot of different things with one language.

 

I also worked out a basic understanding of languages like Go or Rust.
